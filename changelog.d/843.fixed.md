`deliver_after=resume` messages now fire on their own after a bus-delivered session reset, instead of sitting in `deferred` forever. Staging a post-compaction self-handoff (`send --deliver-after=resume`) previously had **no automatic promotion path at all**: the only thing that ever moved such a row to `queued` was an explicit `flush_deferred{trigger:"resume"}`, so a chamber that compacted and simply came back — or was woken by an ordinary bus message — left its own orientation staged indefinitely. The `register` trigger got an auto-fire in #258a ("the register IS the fire"); `resume` never did, and seven staged self-handoffs across two chambers had rotted in `deferred` by the time this was diagnosed, one of them after the diagnosis was filed. The mailman now promotes the agent's `resume`-staged rows when it delivers a session reset (`/compact` or `/clear`) to that agent, right after the post-reset stability window it already waits out — the settled reset being the "chamber went away and came back" edge, and the direct analog of the register auto-fire. Promotion rings the existing delivery doorbell, and `created_at` ordering lands the staged handoff ahead of any traffic that arrived while the chamber was away. The promote is deliberately **not** gated on `--post-compact-pause`: disabling that pause is a delivery-timing choice and must not silently switch off a correctness feature, and the observe / pre-paste gate still holds the paste until the pane is actually paste-safe. Scope is honest — a `/compact` typed directly into a pane leaves no control row for the mailman to observe, so that path still needs the (idempotent) explicit `flush_deferred{trigger:"resume"}`; the CLI, MCP tool descriptions, agent manual and reference now say so instead of presenting the manual flush as the only mechanism.
